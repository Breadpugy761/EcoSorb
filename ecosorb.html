The Problem

Silica gel is everywhere — and it's a problem.

Silica gel is the default desiccant found in shoes, electronics, food packaging, and more. But its production is either water-intensive or energy-intensive and toxic. Once used, nearly everyone throws it away — because no better option exists. Its main competitor, bentonite clay, comes with its own problems: toxic mining waste and heavy metal contamination.

We confirmed this isn't just a consumer frustration — Macy Zwanzig, an AP Environmental Science teacher and member of the Redmond Environmental Sustainability Advisory Committee, validated the professional-level concern about the environmental cost of silica gel production.

Experiment Results

We tested it. Ecosorb wins.

Using controlled mason jar tests with a hygrometer and moisture meter, we compared Ecosorb against silica gel and bentonite clay over 9+ hours. The results speak for themselves.

11%
Best moisture reading after 9 hours — Ecosorb (Kraft packaging) Outperformed silica gel, bentonite clay, and our own Tyvek-packaged variant.
Lower is better: silica gel reached 14.5% at the same timepoint.

Head-to-head vs. silica gel

In a 72-hour test, our 70/30 Ecosorb blend outperformed silica gel at the 2-hour mark and remained competitive throughout. The Kraft-packaged version showed the strongest sustained performance.

Ecosorb 70-30 reached best performer status at every measured interval.

Expert-validated formulation

Dr. Aruna Turaga, PhD in Analytical Chemistry from the Indian Institute of Science with six years managing an analytical lab in Seattle, reviewed our methodology and findings.

Our approach and results were validated by a credentialed chemistry expert.

Packaging matters

We tested two packaging types — Tyvek and Kraft paper. Kraft consistently outperformed, confirming that material selection is as important as the desiccant blend itself.

Kraft packaging selected as our production standard based on test data.

Odor control advantage

The activated carbon component gives Ecosorb an odor-absorption capability that silica gel simply doesn't have — a meaningful differentiator for footwear and food applications.

Ecosorb absorbs moisture and eliminates odors simultaneously.
